Joseph Smith's First Vision

1820

Joseph Smith claims to receive his first vision from God, marking the beginning of the events that would lead to the founding of the Mormon Church.

Founding of the Church of Christ (Later The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints)

1830

Smith publishes the Book of Mormon and formally organizes the Mormon Church in Fayette, New York. The new religion attracts both followers and critics.

Move to Ohio and Missouri

1831 - 1838

The Mormons move to Kirtland, Ohio, where they build a strong religious community. They later settle in Missouri, but tensions arise due to religious differences, political influence, and claims of communal land ownership.

Mormon War in Missouri

1838

Growing hostility between Mormons and Missourians culminates in violent clashes.

Executive Order 44

October 27, 1838

Missouri Governor Lilburn Boggs issues Executive Order 44, also known as the “Extermination Order,” which declares that Mormons must be driven from the state or exterminated.

Hawn's Mill Massacre

October 30th, 1838

By far the bloodiest event in the 1838 Mormon War in Missouri, it occurred when a mob attacked a Mormon settlement, killing 17 men and boys
